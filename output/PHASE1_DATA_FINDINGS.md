## Green lights — findings the report can claim

1. **Revenue is heavily long-tailed.** Top 10% of SKUs = 52.6% of
   revenue; top 20% = 74.5%; top 50% = 95.3%. Empirical foundation for
   the "revenue-weighted" framing in §4, §7, §18.

2. **Walmart dominates revenue ($13.10M, 51.3%) and chargeback dollars
   ($45.83k, 44.3%).** §8 ("True net margin by retailer") still has a
   clean story — Walmart trade spend is $2.79M (21% of Walmart
   revenue); after trade spend and chargebacks Walmart still dwarfs
   every other retailer.

3. **Chargeback Pareto is extreme.**
   - Top 10% of SKUs: **54.4% of chargeback dollars**
   - Top 20%: **79.5%**
   - Top 30%: **87.4%**
   - Top 50%: **96.1%**

   Only 63 of 90 SKUs have any chargebacks. Top ~10 SKUs ≈ 60% of
   chargeback dollars (top 9 = 54.4%, top 18 = 79.5%, interpolated).

4. **Chargeback reasons concentrate on data defects.**
   - Invalid GTIN/UPC: **52.7%** ($54.5k, 192 records)
   - Missing product data: **24.0%** ($24.8k, 112 records)
   - Dimension mismatch: **17.0%** ($17.6k, 70 records)
   - Short shipment: 4.1% ($4.2k)
   - Late delivery: 2.1% ($2.2k)

   Three data-defect reasons account for **93.7%** of chargeback
   dollars. Empirical hook for "SKU-level data quality drives
   chargebacks."

5. **Chargeback exposure tracks specific defects strongly.**

   | defect                  | n_with | mean cb (with) | mean cb (without) | ratio |
   |-------------------------|--------|----------------|-------------------|-------|
   | invalid GTIN            | 9      | $6,251         | $582              | 10.75x|
   | invalid UPC             | 3      | $3,618         | $1,063            | 3.40x |
   | OneWorldSync incomplete | 81     | $1,195         | $729              | 1.64x |
   | missing case weight     | 29     | $1,382         | $1,037            | 1.33x |
   | missing case dims       | 29     | $1,382         | $1,037            | 1.33x |
   | missing brand owner     | 17     | $1,399         | $1,090            | 1.28x |
   | missing country         | 6      | $1,010         | $1,158            | 0.87x |
   | implausible weight      | 71     | $1,067         | $1,455            | 0.73x |

   GTIN-14 invalid is the headline (10.75x). Small n (9) — pair with
   the issue-count regression for the report.

6. **SKU-level quality vs. chargeback correlation.**
   Spearman(quality_score, chargeback_$) = **−0.498**. Quartile
   breakdown:

   | quality quartile | n  | mean cb $ | median cb $ | cb share |

   Bottom-half SKUs (by quality) carry **83.6%** of chargeback dollars.
   §17 hero finding: bad data → more chargeback exposure, with a 6.4x
   mean ratio between worst and best quartile.

## Flags — findings the report should NOT claim

1. **GTIN/UPC validator uses the dataset's algorithm, not strict GS1.**
   Synthetic data was generated with EAN-13-style weights (1,3,1,3,…
   from leftmost) for both GTIN-14 and UPC-A. The dataset-matching
   algorithm flags 9 GTINs and 3 UPCs (the deliberate corruptions);
   strict GS1 would flag ~81% of SKUs. **Methodology appendix must
   call this out** — a real audit would use GS1 strict and ~80%
   invalid would itself be the headline.

2. **GTIN/UPC chargeback signal rests on small n.** The 10.75x and
   3.40x exposure ratios are real and clean, but they're computed on
   9 invalid-GTIN and 3 invalid-UPC SKUs respectively. The §17/§14
   write-up should disclose the n explicitly, or pair the per-defect
   ratio with the (more robust) issue-count regression result.

3. **Deauth correlation is concentrated, not gradient.** Spearman is
   −0.318 but Q1 mean (0.0075) is *below* Q2 mean (0.0162). §12
   should frame as bottom-half-vs-top-half (the clean 4–5× gap),
   not a strict monotonic ladder.

## Things that still look synthetic

- **Time-to-shelf gaps are quantized to 7-day multiples starting at 5**
  (values are exactly {5, 12, 19, 26, 33, 40, 47}). The variance and
  the quality coupling are real, but the discrete grid is an obvious
  artifact of authorized_date snapping and weekly first_scan_week.
  Disclose in methodology; do not claim "continuous" gap distribution.
- **Deauth rate per SKU is on a coarse grid.** With 80 deauths spread
  across ~12.5k authorizations the per-SKU rates are mostly 0 or
  small fractions; the q25 and median in Q3/Q4 are exactly 0. The
  signal is in *whether any deauth occurred*, not in the rate
  magnitude. §12 should lean on count/share-of-SKUs more than
  rate magnitudes.
- **Issue-count histogram is identical to the prior pass:** 1 / 14 /
  35 / 29 / 10 / 1. The product_master defect distribution still
  hasn't been regenerated — only the downstream signals were re-tied
  to it.
- **OneWorldSync incomplete on exactly 90% of SKUs (81/90).** Same
  round number as both prior passes. Either intentional or
  convenient.
- **Equal product-line counts (30/30/30).** Unchanged.
- **Computable-promo-lift fraction stuck at 18/125 (14.4%).** Same
  exact number as last pass. Promotions still appear front-loaded in
  the early scan window; suspicious for a 2-year window.
- **`weight_plausible` flag direction is mildly anomalous** (SKUs with
  implausible weights have *fewer* chargebacks: 0.73x). Likely an
  artifact of how `weight_plausible == NA` (when case_weight_lbs is
  missing) interacts with `issue_count` — only strict-FALSE counts as
  an issue, but ~32% of SKUs are NA on this check. Methodology note,
  not a story problem.
- **Retailer readiness numbers are pixel-identical across all three
  refreshes** (44.4 / 46.7 / 54.4 / 70.0). Pattern is plausible but
  the exact stability across refreshes suggests the rule-evaluation
  output is deterministic on an unchanged product_master.
